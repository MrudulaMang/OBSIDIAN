how to learn fast 1.30 hr lesson

## 🧠 Technical answer (no fluff)

You don’t “watch” a 1.5 hr lesson — you **compress it actively**.

---

## ⚡ Fast-learning method (DevOps-friendly)

### 🔹 1. Speed + scan (30–40 min)

- Watch at **1.5x–2x**
- Don’t pause too much
- Capture only:
    - commands
    - concepts
    - errors

👉 Goal: **build mental map, not detail**

---

### 🔹 2. Rebuild from memory (20–25 min)

Close video. Now:

write:  
- what problem?  
- what steps?  
- what commands?

👉 If you can’t recall → that’s your gap

---

### 🔹 3. Hands-on (30–40 min) 🔥

Run everything:

kubectl ...  
aws ...  
docker ...

Break it intentionally:

- wrong config
- wrong port
- missing permission

👉 This is where real learning happens

---

### 🔹 4. Create cheat sheet (10 min)

Example:

kubectl get pods  
kubectl describe pod  
kubectl logs

👉 Your **own notes > video**

---

## 🔥 Compression formula

|Step|Time|
|---|---|
|Watch|40 min|
|Recall|20 min|
|Hands-on|40 min|
|Notes|10 min|

👉 Total ≈ 1.5–2 hrs → but **deep understanding**

---

## ❌ What NOT to do

- Passive watching ❌
- Rewatching multiple times ❌
- Copy-paste without thinking ❌

---

## 🧠 DevOps mindset

> **“If you didn’t run it, you didn’t learn it.”**

---

## ⚡ One-line takeaway

> **Watch fast → recall → execute → break → note**
