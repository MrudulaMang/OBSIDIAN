A common set of challenges includes maintaining Jenkins plugins, since they require frequent updates and can introduce compatibility issues. You also have scaling difficulties when you move from a handful of jobs to hundreds, especially if you haven’t set up distributed builds or robust agents. Managing credentials securely can become a pain point if not handled properly. Pipeline debugging can be tricky, because failures might come from scripts, environment issues, or external integrations. Finally, ensuring proper access control—who can configure pipelines, view logs, or trigger builds—often requires careful setup, especially in larger teams. In short, keeping Jenkins stable, secure, and scalable requires ongoing effort and attention to detail.
