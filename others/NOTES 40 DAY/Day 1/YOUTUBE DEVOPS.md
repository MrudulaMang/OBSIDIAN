BUT I AM NOT AN EXPERT YET, CAN I still create content

Yes — **you can absolutely create content even if you are not an expert yet.** In fact, many successful tech YouTube channels started exactly that way.

For your situation, this can actually become a **strength**.

---

# 1. You don't need to be an expert to teach

You only need to be **one step ahead of the viewer**.

Example:

If you just learned:

- `top`
    
- `ps`
    
- `strace`
    
- `lsof`
    

You can explain:

> “When I first tried debugging CPU spikes, I discovered these commands.”

Many viewers are **beginners too**.

---

# 2. Learning-in-public works very well

A very powerful content style is:

**“Learning DevOps publicly.”**

Example titles:

- “Today I learned how `strace` helps debug stuck processes”
    
- “Understanding `lsof` while debugging port issues”
    
- “What I learned investigating a CPU spike in Linux”
    

This feels **authentic** and relatable.

---

# 3. Experts often explain poorly

Beginners remember the **confusion points**.

Experts sometimes forget what was difficult.

You can explain things like:

- “This confused me initially”
    
- “Here is the mistake I made”
    
- “Here is the command that finally helped”
    

This makes content **very relatable**.

---

# 4. Many big creators started as learners

Several tech creators built audiences while learning:

- documenting experiments
    
- explaining concepts they just learned
    
- sharing debugging attempts
    

Over time, their **knowledge and channel grew together**.

---

# 5. Be honest with viewers

You can say things like:

> “I'm learning DevOps and documenting what I discover.”

People respect this honesty.

---

# 6. The key rule

Never **pretend to know everything**.

Instead say:

- “Here is what I learned”
    
- “Here is how this command works”
    
- “Here is the debugging path I followed”
    

That builds **trust**.

---

# 7. This actually fits your goal perfectly

Your career positioning is:

- systems thinker
    
- debugging mindset
    
- root-cause oriented
    

A channel where you **investigate systems step by step** matches this perfectly.

Example video idea:

Simulating a production CPU spike  
Step 1: Check top  
Step 2: Find PID with ps  
Step 3: Inspect with strace  
Step 4: Check open files with lsof

This kind of **investigation video** is rare and valuable.

## 1. Beginners explain in simpler language

Experts sometimes explain things assuming the viewer already knows a lot.

Example:

Expert explanation:

> “This fails because of container orchestration scheduling constraints.”

Beginner explanation:

> “Kubernetes couldn’t place the pod because the node didn’t have enough CPU.”

Most viewers on YouTube are **beginners**, so they prefer the **simpler explanation**.
