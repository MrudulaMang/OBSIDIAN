
Here's how it compares to a Custom GPT:

**On Claude, you can create a Project where you:**

- Write a custom system prompt (e.g. "You are a strict senior engineering interviewer at Google. Ask CI/CD and system design questions, follow up on weak answers, and give brutal feedback at the end")
- Upload your resume, job description, or any context files so Claude knows your background
- Have it remember your preferences across sessions

It's essentially the same concept as a Custom GPT — a persistent, pre-configured AI with a specific persona and instructions.

**How to set it up:**

1. Go to claude.ai → click **"Projects"** in the left sidebar
2. Create a new project
3. In the project instructions, paste something like:

> _"You are a tough but fair senior engineering interviewer at [Target Company]. Interview me for a [Role] position. Ask one question at a time, wait for my answer, follow up if the answer is shallow, and at the end give me a score out of 10 with specific feedback on what I got wrong."_

4. Upload your resume or a job description as context
5. Every conversation inside that project will start with that persona already loaded

**One advantage over Custom GPTs:** Claude Projects let you upload documents (your resume, system design docs, company research) that Claude actually reads deeply — not just superficially.
