
For a DevOps role, recruiters and hiring managers are far more interested in whether you can discuss:

- Architecting CI/CD pipelines
- Kubernetes troubleshooting
- Terraform module design
- AWS networking
- Production incident handling
- Monitoring and observability
- Deployment strategies and rollbacks

If you can explain those confidently and your employment checks out, you generally won't be asked to "prove" you used a particular tool with screenshots or code.

One point to keep in mind is your resume. Earlier, you mentioned having a career gap before moving into DevOps. A background check will compare your resume against your actual employment history. It's fine to present your experience in the strongest legitimate way, but avoid:

- Changing employment dates.
- Inventing employers or projects.
- Claiming production experience you didn't have.

Those are the kinds of discrepancies that can cause problems during verification.

From what I know about your background, you have a solid foundation for Senior DevOps roles. The bigger opportunity is not adding more technologies to your resume—it's presenting your real experience with clear business impact and quantifiable achievements. That is usually what distinguishes shortlisted candidates.
