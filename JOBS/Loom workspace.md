In Loom, **“recording into a workspace”** means choosing **which team space (workspace) your video gets saved to**.

### Simple example

Suppose you are part of:

- Personal Workspace
    
- Company Workspace
    
- Client Workspace
    

When you click **Record** in Loom, the video must be stored somewhere.

That destination is your **default Workspace**.

Loom currently records only into your **default workspace**, so if you want the recording saved to your company team instead of personal space, you must switch your default workspace first. 

---

### What a Workspace is

A Workspace is like a shared area where:

- videos are stored
    
- team members can access them
    
- permissions/privacy are managed
    
- folders and spaces are organized
    

Think of it like a Google Drive folder + team access control.

---

### “Record into Workspace” means

➡ Your screen recording + camera recording gets automatically saved inside that selected workspace.

Not just on your laptop.

It becomes available there for:

- sharing
    
- comments
    
- team review
    
- permissions control
    
- editing later
    

---

### Example in office use

If you're recording:

- production issue explanation
    
- deployment demo
    
- outage RCA walkthrough
    
- Kubernetes troubleshooting demo
    

you’d usually want it saved in your **team/company workspace**, not personal.

---

### How to change it

Loom says you can change it using:

1. the **Workspace switcher** (top left)
    
2. **My Profile → Workspace settings**
    

before starting the recording. 

---

So short answer:

**Recording Loom workspace = choosing where your Loom video will live and who can access it.**

  

does it cost

  

Yes — **Loom has both free and paid plans**.

### Pricing (current)

|Plan|Cost|Main limits|
|---|---|---|
|Starter|Free ($0)|25 videos/person, 5 min per recording|
|Business|$18/user/month|Unlimited videos + unlimited recording time|
|Business + AI|$24/user/month|Includes AI editing + summaries + advanced features|
|Enterprise|Custom pricing|Large teams, SSO, advanced security|

This is based on Loom’s official pricing and Atlassian support pages. 

---

### For “workspace recording”

If you're just recording into a workspace:

- **Personal/basic use** → Free plan may be enough
    
- **Team/company shared workspace with unlimited recording** → usually paid plan
    
- **Advanced admin/security/company controls** → Enterprise
    

Even the free Starter plan includes team workspace features, but with limits like 5-minute recordings and 25 videos/person.

### What you can record

### 1. Screen recording

Record your:

- full screen
    
- one application window
    
- browser tab
    

Example:

- showing a Kubernetes deployment issue
    
- explaining Jenkins pipeline failure
    
- demo of Terraform code
    
- walkthrough of AWS console setup
    

---

### 2. Camera recording

Just your webcam video.

Example:

- introduction video
    
- interview follow-up
    
- client update
    
- team update
    

---

### 3. Screen + Camera together

Most common use.

Example:

- teaching something while your face appears in a small bubble
    
- outage RCA explanation
    
- architecture walkthrough
    

This is great for DevOps demos.

---

### 4. Microphone audio

You explain while recording.

Example:

- “This pod failed because readiness probe was misconfigured…”
    

---

### 5. Instant troubleshooting videos

Instead of typing:

“it is failing”

you can show:

- logs
    
- terminal
    
- error
    
- expected behavior
    

Much faster.
